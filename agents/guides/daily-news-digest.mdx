## What This Agent Does

Searches for news on topics you care about and emails you a summary every morning. No more scrolling through news sites—get the highlights delivered automatically.

**Example output:** Every day at 8am, you receive an email with the top 5 AI industry stories from the past 24 hours.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the agent">
    From the homepage, enter this prompt:
    
    ```
    Every morning at 8am, search for news about artificial intelligence 
    from the last 24 hours. Find the top 5 most relevant stories. 
    Email me a summary with headlines, sources, and a 2-sentence 
    description of each story. Send to [your-email@example.com].
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize the topic">
    In the chat, tell Incredible what you want to track:
    - "Focus on AI startups and funding news"
    - "Include news about OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google AI"
    - "Skip opinion pieces, only factual news"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set the schedule">
    Click **Add Trigger** (⚡) → **Schedule** → Daily at 8:00 AM.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test and enable">
    Click **Play** (▶️) to test. Check your inbox. If it looks good, click **Enable** on your schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example Prompt

```
Every morning at 8am, search for news about [YOUR TOPIC] from the last 24 hours.

Find the top 5 most relevant stories. For each story include:
- Headline
- Source name
- 2-sentence summary
- Link to the article

Email the digest to [YOUR EMAIL] with subject "Daily [TOPIC] News - [DATE]".

Skip opinion pieces and focus on factual news from reputable sources.
```

## Variations

| Use Case | Modification |
|----------|-------------|
| **Competitor news** | "Search for news about [Company A], [Company B], and [Company C]" |
| **Industry trends** | "Search for news about [industry] trends, funding, and product launches" |
| **Weekly instead of daily** | Change schedule to weekly on Monday mornings |
| **Slack instead of email** | "Post the summary to #news-updates on Slack" |

## Tips

<Tip>
**Be specific about sources.** Adding "from TechCrunch, The Verge, and Reuters" improves relevance.
</Tip>

- Include your email address in the prompt
- Specify the number of stories you want
- Define what "relevant" means to you (recent funding, product launches, etc.)
